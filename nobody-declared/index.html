The Making Of

1 · The Starting Point

As a design form, it has existed for over a century. From hand-drawn illustration to photography to digital compositing, visual language has continued to evolve. This project is an attempt — to add the dimension of time to a poster, and to explore how the AI era can reimagine poster design through motion.

2 · Tools Used

  • Claude Copywriting & concept development
  • Nano Banana Static image generation
  • Photoshop Minor image adjustments & typography
  • Seedance Image-to-motion animation
  • Claude Code Website production

3 · The Process

Starting from the concept of a motion poster / movie poster, the primary visual style draws on halftone dots, Risograph Printing, and related aesthetics to create imagery that feels cinematic and story-driven.

3-1  Every film still is the result of multiple generation rounds — continuously refined and iterated.

3-2  Realistic stills were first generated, then further transformed using Nano Banana Pro.

3-3  Prompts Used

Vintage Cinematic Look

A high-definition, cinematic still with a realistic photographic quality. The medium features a shallow depth of field, sharply focusing on the foreground while the background is softly blurred. Vintage organic film texture, visible grain structure, nostalgic warm tones, handheld documentary feel, retro analog look, subtle color shifts, natural imperfections, film stock character, practical lighting captured on emulsion, textured cinematic quality.

Misregistered Print Effect

Change to screen-print style, Electric Orange and Teal color layers slightly misregistered. A bold graphic poster illustration of a mob boss character, extreme flat graphic design style, strong geometric shapes, Risograph print separation effect, halftone texture, screen printing aesthetic, high contrast black dominant, sharp silhouette.

Failed Tests

During style conversion, the use of halftone and misregistered print effects occasionally produced moiré patterns — those images were discarded.

3-4  Concept Short Film

Taking the motion poster concept further, multiple video clips were generated with Seedance 2 and brought together through editing into a single, cohesive short film.

4 · The Final Thought

In a traditional design workflow, every revision costs time and effort — so many ideas get abandoned before they're ever tried. AI has brought the cost of trial and error down dramatically. We can now explore 20 directions in a single afternoon and choose the one that works. That was simply not possible before. It's not just about efficiency — it's about expanding the range of visual possibilities.

Writing prompts, it turns out, is an exercise in clarity. When a designer uses AI to assist with visual creation, they have to translate the image in their mind into a prompt — telling the AI what they want to see. You have to know what you're looking for before you can ask for it.

Although this is a fictional project, every decision was still intentional, every result still earned. Nothing arrived fully formed — each outcome was the product of many iterations, many discarded directions. AI expands what's possible. What remains is always a human choice.
